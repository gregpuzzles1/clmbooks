**Author:** Ron Christian

## Summary

*Mountain Peaks of Salvation History* is a Christian devotional and biblical survey by Ron Christian that traces major moments in God's redemptive work from the Exodus to the Incarnation. The book presents the Ten Commandments as God's unchanging moral standard and shows how Israel's history, the Davidic monarchy, and the ministry of the prophets point forward to Christ. Christian maintains that God reveals both mercy and judgment through His dealings with Israel, calling His people away from idolatry, social injustice, fear, and misplaced trust in human power. The chapters on Amos, Micah, and Nahum contend that God's love is never sentimental or detached from justice, but includes warning, correction, accountability, restoration, and mercy. The final chapter proposes that Jesus is the focus of all salvation history, fulfilling Old Testament prophecy not through political conquest but through humble, redemptive self-giving in the Incarnation. Overall, the book invites readers to see Scripture as one unfolding story of God's righteous character, His covenant purposes, and His saving work through Christ.
